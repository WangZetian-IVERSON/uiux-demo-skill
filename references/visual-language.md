# Visual Language Rules

## Distilled Aesthetic

Dark-tech UI/UX portfolio style:

- Dark cinematic background.
- Electric blue as the main accent.
- White oversized Chinese titles paired with compact futuristic English subtitles.
- Device mockups, glass panels, floating metric cards, and 3D UI objects.
- Grid floors, depth shadows, glow edges, and spotlight composition.
- Screens treated as physical objects, not flat images.

Warm commercial UI/UX portfolio style:

- Orange/yellow gradients over dark or light cream surfaces.
- Friendly 3D mascot/object covers.
- Ecommerce app screenshot collages.
- Large playful Chinese titles with strong commercial keywords.
- Light product-review pages with business goals and efficiency claims.
- Icon/component library pages with soft rounded tiles.
- Campaign poster galleries and operations visual appendices.
- B-side SaaS/official site pages with laptop mockups and cobalt accents.

## Palette

Base:
Use near-black, blue-black, or deep navy surfaces. Avoid pure flat black when a textured, radial, or gridded background would add depth.

Accent:
Use saturated blue or cyan for primary emphasis. Purple, orange, mint, or yellow can appear as secondary category accents, but do not let them overwhelm the blue-tech identity unless the project domain demands it.

Commercial accent:
For ecommerce/retail/service projects, orange can become the primary accent. Pair it with cream, pale yellow, soft mint, warm gray, and small cobalt details. Keep orange tied to business actions: price, promotion, CTA, category emphasis, or conversion.

Text:
Use white for primary headings, blue for selected English words or keywords, and cool gray for metadata/body copy.

Glow:
Use glow for focus, active states, depth separation, and data energy. Keep glow controlled; too many glowing elements make the page cheap.

## Typography

Chinese titles:
Use bold, square, modern type. Titles should feel engineered and confident.

Commercial Chinese titles:
Use heavier, playful, poster-like Chinese titles when the project is ecommerce, operations, or campaign-driven. Color split the title to emphasize the business keyword.

English labels:
Use condensed, futuristic, or wide display type for labels like `Design Portfolio`, `Core Page Front`, `Vision System`, `Competitive Analysis`.

Hierarchy:
Use large page title, medium section label, compact body copy, and tiny metadata. Make the title the first read, the product mockup the second read, and the explanation the third read.

## Layout

Use asymmetric balance. A common structure is:

```text
Left: title + reasoning
Right: large device/product proof
Edges: year/contact/section metadata
Background: grid, texture, or spatial lighting
```

For dense analysis pages:
Use clear containers, dashed method boxes, arrows, or split columns. Do not let dense text float without structure.

For visual proof pages:
Let mockups breathe. Use scale, perspective, and layering to make the page feel designed.

## Spatial Treatment

Screenshots should be integrated through:

- Phone or desktop frames.
- Angled perspective.
- Rounded glass panels.
- Shadows and contact lighting.
- Reflections, glow edges, or background cards.
- Callouts that point to real UI decisions.

Avoid pasting raw rectangular screenshots without treatment unless the page is explicitly a page excerpt/gallery.

## Common Motifs

- Perspective grid floor.
- Floating 3D cards for contents.
- Phone mockup on a dark pedestal or glass panel.
- Blue metric cards hovering above screenshots.
- Dashboard panels arranged like a command center.
- Map/digital twin backgrounds for power, city, IoT, and enterprise systems.
- Bilingual labels with one English keyword highlighted in blue.
- Small top-edge metadata: `2025 Design Portfolio`, email, section path.

## Motion / Interaction If Building HTML

Use restrained motion:

- Stagger section entrance.
- Slow parallax on floating cards.
- Subtle glow pulse on active data points.
- Device mockup lift on hover.
- Section transition that feels like moving through a digital portfolio space.

Avoid constant busy animation. The portfolio should feel premium and controlled.

## Readability Rules

- Keep body copy high contrast.
- Do not place small gray text over busy textures.
- Maintain consistent page margins.
- Use no more than 2-3 type styles per deck.
- Ensure all callouts are close to the UI element they describe.
- If exporting to PPT or images, verify text is readable at presentation size.

## Domain Variations

Mobile civic/service apps:
Keep dark portfolio packaging but let the product UI remain clean, bright, and approachable. Use blue service accents and clear task-entry explanations.

Ecommerce and marketplace apps:
Use warm commercial energy, screenshot mosaics, promotion modules, product-card detail callouts, order/payment flow evidence, and business value metrics. Do not let the page become a poster collection without UX reasoning.

B-side SaaS and admin systems:
Use light product evidence, cobalt accents, desktop mockups, component boards, KPI cards, and clean callouts. Focus on efficiency, consistency, and information architecture.

Operations visuals and campaign posters:
Use dark gallery pages or warm light grids to show range. Always state the campaign goal or channel so the work reads as applied design, not detached graphic decoration.

Icon/component systems:
Use soft white cards, repeated tiles, generous spacing, and category labels. Explain customization, reuse, and consistency rules.

Sleep, wellness, or lifestyle apps:
Allow dreamlike illustration backgrounds, soft purple/blue palettes, and emotional onboarding pages. Still preserve portfolio logic and readable case-study structure.

Industrial, energy, city, or command systems:
Use deeper backgrounds, map textures, digital twin visuals, electric blue panels, and operational data language.

Brand/VI pages:
Use calmer grids and larger object photography/mockups. The portfolio wrapper remains dark, but the brand work can bring its own color system.
